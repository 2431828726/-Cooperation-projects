First Aid and Injury Management

During an earthquake, first aid skills can help us manage injuries and assist the injured. First and foremost, learning cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) is essential to assist someone whose heart has stopped beating when needed. Secondly, understanding how to handle common injuries such as bleeding, fractures, and burns is crucial. Learn the correct methods for bandaging and stopping bleeding to reduce the risk of infection and bleeding. Additionally, know how to use and where to find first aid supplies, such as first aid kits and life jackets.

After an earthquake, emergency situations can lead to various types of injuries. Therefore, understanding first aid measures for different situations is crucial. For example, if someone is choking or having difficulty breathing, take immediate measures for artificial respiration or other emergency assistance. For someone experiencing cardiac arrest, perform CPR promptly to increase their chances of survival. At the same time, if someone has suffered a severe fracture or trauma, stabilize their condition and transport them to the hospital as quickly as possible.

After an earthquake, many people may experience psychological trauma and difficulties. Therefore, providing psychological support and emergency psychological care is essential. First, communicate with the injured or affected individuals, expressing concern and support. Listen to their feelings and provide emotional support and comfort. Secondly, offer appropriate psychological assistance resources, such as professional psychological counseling and therapy, to help people cope with trauma and rebuild inner stability.

Through the content of first aid and injury management above, it is hoped that readers can enhance their emergency capabilities during earthquakes, effectively manage injuries, provide first aid assistance, and pay attention to mental health. This includes offering necessary support and care for the injured and affected individuals.
