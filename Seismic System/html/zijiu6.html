Water, Food, and Supplies Reserve

During an earthquake, the water supply system may be interrupted or damaged, so it is crucial to have an adequate reserve of drinking water. Each household should store at least three days' worth of drinking water, with a recommended minimum of 1 gallon (approximately 3.8 liters) per person per day. You can purchase bottled water or prepare enough water storage containers, keeping the water in a dry, clean place, and regularly check and replace stored water.

In addition to water, having a sufficient supply of food is also essential. Choose nutritious, easily storable, and edible foods, such as canned goods, dried fruits, nuts, energy bars, etc. Consider the specific needs of family members, including special diets, baby food, and pet food. Ensure that food is stored in a dry, cool place and regularly check and replace expired or spoiled items.

Aside from water and food, it's also important to reserve some necessary supplies and tools to handle emergencies after an earthquake. These items include flashlights, spare batteries, mobile phone chargers, first aid kits, spare clothing and blankets, a portable radio, matches or lighters, a toolkit, and more. Place these items in easily accessible locations, and ensure that every family member knows their location and how to use them.

By properly storing water, food, and supplies, we can better cope with emergencies after an earthquake and ensure that the basic needs of the family are met. These preparations will provide a safe and comfortable environment and increase our ability to withstand disasters. Remember to regularly check and update stored supplies to ensure their availability and quality.
